Thank you for visiting my portfolio, which is currently under construction! I am a burgeoning Data Scientist with a drive to deliver understable answers to all parties involved: I graduated from the University of Vermont with a BA in Mathematics (Pure) before I began a career in Banking. As an inquisitive data scientist, I naturally search for answers. My detail-oriented mindset allows me to break down complex problems into manageable parts to which I apply problem solving and analytical skills. While the answer is important to me, it is more important to understand why I have come to an answer, so that I can share my results with others. Using both technical and interpersonal skills I want to help companies help their consumers arrive at the right solutions and know why the solution is correct.

Reddit NLP

As someone who plays Dungeons and Dragons, I was curious if the topics D&D players and Dungeon Masters discuss are the same or similar. This project explores Natural Language Processing, using Reddit comments and submissions to draw conclusions about specific subreddits. I explored the subreddits ‘dndnext’ and ‘dmacademy’ to draw inferences about the users and built a model to determine if an individual was posting to the subreddit for D&D players or the subreddit for D&D Dungeon Masters.

Daylight Savings and Traffic Implications

This project was the first Data Science project I completed in a group setting. Together with my cohorts Curtis HopeHill and Jeffrey Floyd, we explored whether the frequency and severity of traffic accidents were greater in the periods immediately following the changes to and from Daylight Savings Time.

I was primarily responsible for planning the project as well as Data Cleaning and Visualizations.

Circus Tent

In the time of COVID-19 Live Music has been sorely missed in my life. For my DSI Capstone, I wanted to explore a topic I was passionate about and I choose to explore the previous live performance of the jam band Phish. My goal was to build a neural network to predict the band's upcoming setlists, based on all prior performances. This project demonstrates the full 'data science process', from data collection to cleaning and visualization and ultimately modeling and conclusions.
